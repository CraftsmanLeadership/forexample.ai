## Understanding Coral Reef Monitoring
Coral reef monitoring is crucial for understanding the health and resilience of these ecosystems. Traditional methods involve manual surveys, which can be time-consuming, labor-intensive, and often limited to small areas. AI can revolutionize this process by analyzing satellite and drone imagery, underwater videos, and sensor data to track changes in coral health, water quality, and marine life.

## How AI Works for Coral Reef Monitoring
AI algorithms can be trained to detect and classify coral species, identify bleaching events, and monitor water quality parameters. For instance, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) can be used to analyze satellite imagery and detect changes in coral cover and health. Another approach is using object detection algorithms to identify and count marine species in underwater videos.

## Real-World Examples
One notable example is the use of AI-powered drones to monitor coral reefs in the Great Barrier Reef. These drones equipped with high-resolution cameras and AI algorithms can detect coral bleaching, identify marine life, and track changes in water quality.
